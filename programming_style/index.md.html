**Elements of Programming Style** Stijn Heymans # Introduction This article is taking a different approach than we have before in our other [elements of](./elements.html) articles. Back in ancient times, probably around 2003-2004, I found Strunk and White's [The Elements of Style](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/33514.The_Elements_of_Style). A classic style manual for improving your writing, formulated as non-negotiable rules. The year 2021 will go into history as the year of the P. The year I discovered [The Elements of Programming Style](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/454039.The_Elements_of_Programming_Style?ac=1&from_search=true&qid=oWF4aKlBCP&rank=1) by Brian W. Kernighan and P.J. Plauger. _The Elements of Programming Style_ sets out to do for programming what _The Elements of Style_ did for writing: a set of guidelines to follow when programming, resulting in more readable code (versus more readable prose). In fact, Kernighan and Plauger, refer to _The Elements of Style_ explicitly where they "tried to emulate their brevity by concentrating on the essential practical aspects of style". Why are you reading this? Why did I write this? "What a useless endeavor. Even in the grand scheme of useleness, it's deeply unsettling how useless this is." I see the reader has a sense of drama. Welcome, you've come to the right place. _The Elements of Programming Style_ was first published in 1974 and all code examples of bad style and good style are all in [Fortran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fortran) and [PL/I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PL/I). While interesting historically, this makes the bar for new readers high. This article sets out to do a reading of the original book and chapter by chapter will try come up with modernized examples of Kernighan and Plauger's rules. I'll pick [Ruby](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ruby_(programming_language) as the choice of modern language as it's widely used industry (e.g., at [Shopify](https://www.shopify.com)) and should make sense to a lot of developers living in the year 2020 (and maybe a bit beyond).
